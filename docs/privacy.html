Privacy Policy

The Tower Analyzer — Last updated: June 23, 2026

Overview

The Tower Analyzer ("the app") helps players of The Tower analyze their in-game battle reports. This policy explains what data the app collects, how it is stored, and how you can delete it.

Data Collected

  • Player ID — a unique identifier you enter manually in the app's settings. It is used solely to store and retrieve your own reports.
  • Battle report text — the raw battle report copied from The Tower and submitted through the Android app or iOS Shortcut. This text contains in-game statistics only (wave reached, gold earned, etc.) and no personal information.

No name, email address, device identifier, location, or other personal information is collected.

How Data Is Used

Battle reports are parsed to extract gameplay statistics (run type, wave, gold, kills, etc.) and stored in your account in the analyzer database. They are not used for advertising or any purpose other than displaying your own history to you.

As the developer, I may access submitted reports and metadata to diagnose technical issues or improve the service. This access is limited to what is necessary to maintain the app and is never used for advertising or shared with third parties.

Where Data Is Stored

  • AWS S3 (us-east-2, Ohio) — submitted battle reports and database backups are stored as objects under a path prefix that corresponds to your Player ID. Only you and the developer can access your data.
  • AWS DynamoDB (us-east-2, Ohio) — a small metadata record tracks your current version and the timestamp of your last submission.
  • Local SQLite database — parsed report data is stored in an on-device database (%APPDATA%\TheTowerAnalyzer\analyzer.db on Windows). This file never leaves your device except when you explicitly use the backup feature.

All data is transmitted over HTTPS. Data stored in AWS is encrypted at rest (SSE-S3).

Data Retention

  • Battle reports (S3), account metadata (DynamoDB), and the most recent database backup are retained indefinitely. They are not automatically deleted. To have this data removed, you must submit a deletion request by email (see below).
  • Older database backups (S3) — when a new backup is created, the previous backup is automatically deleted after 30 days via an S3 lifecycle rule.
  • Local SQLite database is retained on your device until you uninstall the app or delete the file manually.

Data Sharing

Your data is never sold, rented, or shared with any third party. It is not used for advertising or marketing purposes.

Your Rights & Deletion

You can delete your data at any time:

  • Individual reports — use the delete button in the analyzer UI.
  • All cloud data (S3 reports, database backups, and DynamoDB record) — email draukadin@gmail.com with your Player ID and a deletion request. All data associated with your Player ID will be removed within 30 days. Without a deletion request, this data is retained indefinitely.

Children

The Tower Analyzer is not directed at children under 13. It does not knowingly collect any information from children.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes materially, the "Last updated" date above will be revised. Continued use of the app after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
