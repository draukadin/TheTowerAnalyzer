# Labs

## How Labs Work

Labs are coin-funded research slots that permanently improve every aspect of your tower —
damage, economy, survivability, UWs, modules, cards, and perks.

**Slots**: you start with one slot, unlocked at T1W30. Additional slots cost gems:

| Slot | Cost |
|------|------|
| 1 | Free (T1W30) |
| 2 | 100 Gems |
| 3 | 400 Gems |
| 4 | 1,400 Gems |
| 5 | 3,000 Gems |

All five slots are equivalent — any lab can go in any slot. Unlock all five as soon as
possible; the gem cost is the best long-term investment in the game. Do not prioritize
card slot unlocks, card copies, or module draws over lab slots.

**Cost and cancellation**: starting a lab costs coins upfront. Research then runs over
time. If you cancel a lab at any point — even with 5 seconds remaining — you get 100%
of your coins back. There is no penalty for stopping and switching.

**Speed**: four things affect how fast labs complete:
1. **Elite cells** — yellow triangles dropped by vampires, scatters, and rays. Applied
   per-slot to set a speed multiplier. The primary daily resource for lab acceleration.
2. **Lab Speed lab** — reduces research time on every other lab. Compounds with everything.
3. **Lab Speed relics** — permanent flat improvement to lab speed.
4. **Gem rush** — instant completion at gem cost. Use sparingly; reserve gems for slots.

**Categories**: Main, Attack, Defense, Utility, Ultimate Weapons, Cards, Perks, Bots,
Enemies, Modules, Battle Condition. Labs within each category are unlocked progressively
by Tier/Wave milestone.

---

## The Most Important Principle: Lab Speed Compounds Everything

**Lab Speed is the highest ROI lab in the game at every stage.** Every level reduces
research time on every other lab running now and forever. The earlier you invest in Lab
Speed, the more total research time you save across your entire account lifetime.

Common mistake: players get Lab Speed to ~50 and move on, treating it as a mid-priority
lab. It has 99 levels and the value of each level is the sum of all future research time
saved. Take it significantly higher than feels necessary — many top players run it to max.

---

## The "Too Long" Heuristic

The mistake is not researching too many levels of something — it is staying in a lab when
the time-per-level exceeds what another lab would return. When a lab level that used to
take 2 days now takes 3 weeks, the opportunity cost of not switching is 3 weeks of
progress on a higher-ROI lab. The question is always: **what does the next level of this
lab give me vs. what does the next level of the best alternative give me?**

Labs where this triggers most often:
- **Defense%** past level 23: strong early, diminishing returns afterward. The marginal
  defense gain from level 24+ is small relative to the coin cost. Exception: when pushing
  for the 98% hard cap in late tournament builds, levels 24+ become relevant again.
- **Defense Absolute** past T1 farming: scales poorly against enemy damage in later tiers.
  A good lab to get to ~10–15 during T1 farming; retire it after moving up.
- **Interest** and **Recovery Package Amount**: broadly considered low-ROI at all stages.
  Other economy labs provide better return per research-hour.

## Stage-Based Priority Guide

### Brand New (T1–T2)

The lab list is short at this stage — unlock everything available rather than being
selective. Two labs to prioritize above all others:

**Game Speed** — takes the game from ×1.5 to ×5 speed at max. This directly multiplies
coins per day: a run that would take 2+ days at ×1.5 takes ~10 hours at high speed. Gold
box this as quickly as possible. It is the single biggest CPH lever available to a new
player.

**Lab Speed** — start it early and return to it often. See above.

After those two, "spread the love": get everything available to a baseline of ~10 levels.
Coins Per Kill, Cash Bonus (essential while workshop upgrades are expensive), Attack Speed,
Garlic Thorns (makes vampires a non-issue starting T2), and Light Speed Shots (projectile
travel time → near-instant, valuable for blender and knockback builds).

---

### Early Game (T3–T8)

More labs unlock; start being selective based on build type.

**EHP builds**:
- Health (~25–30 levels is the early target)
- Health Regen
- Defense% to level 23 (meaningful diminishing returns after)
- Defense Absolute (~10–15 for T1 farming, reduce priority after)

**GC / Hybrid builds**:
- Damage, Crit Factor, Attack Speed — the "big three" that scale throughout the game

**All builds**:
- Coins Per Kill Bonus — economy foundation, valuable at all stages
- Golden Tower Bonus / BH Coin Bonus — if these UWs are unlocked, start early
- **Unlock Perks** (milestone lab) — perks are gamechanging; this is a mandatory unlock
- **First Perk Choice** — lets you select Perk Wave Requirement as your first perk
- **Perk Option Quantity** — unlocks 4 perk choices per wave; reduces RNG on perk draws
- **Recovery Package After Boss** — guarantees a package on every boss wave; strong
  passive survivability for players relying on package health
- **Common Module Drop Chance** (10 levels, short lab) — start early, finish quickly
- **Rare Module Drop Chance** (10 levels, short lab) — same; don't neglect module labs

---

### Late Early Game (T8–T10)

Economy becomes the primary focus. UW coin labs are the biggest CPH lever at this stage.

**UW Economy labs** (max these as quickly as possible):
- Death Wave Cell Bonus
- Death Wave Coin Bonus
- Black Hole Coin Bonus
- Golden Tower Coin Bonus
- Golden Tower Duration
- Spotlight Coin Bonus (if SL is unlocked)

**Perk labs**:
- **Standard Perk Bonus** — applies only to blue standard perks (Defense%, Perk Wave
  Requirement, Game Speed, Cash/Coin bonuses). Does not affect UW perks or trade-off
  perks. Even so, mandatory — the perks it buffs are core run perks.
- **Improve Trade-Off Perks** (10 levels, expensive late levels) — increases the positive
  side of trade-off perks. High value for builds that lean on damage or econ trade-offs.
- **Band Perks** — removes specific unwanted standard perks from the draw pool. Target
  3–4 bands at minimum. Priority targets: perks you never want to see (Range in farming,
  low-tier defense perks once defense is capped).

**Card labs** (baseline levels):
- Orb Adjuster (T6W50 — adjusts extra orb distance; required for optimal BH/orb setups)
- Extra Orb range
- Second Wind Blast

**Module labs**: Daily Mission Shards, Reroll Shards, Module Shards — get baseline levels.

---

### Early Mid Game (T10–T12)

Still chasing economy first; attack labs become co-priority.

**Economy**: finish any UW coin labs not yet maxed. Coins Per Kill Bonus higher.

**Attack labs**:
- Damage, Crit Factor, Attack Speed — continue pushing
- Super Crit Chance and Super Crit Multiplier — start investing
- Enemy Attack Level Skips and Enemy Health Level Skips (20 levels each): get to at
  least level 10 (unlocks +1% skip). Finish both eventually — valuable at all stages.

**Module labs** (highest priority in this category):
- **Shatter Shards** (5 levels, long and expensive) — start immediately when affordable.
  Every level adds 20% to shard yield from all shattered modules. The sooner you start,
  the more shards you earn before it completes. Don't wait.
- Daily Mission Shards, Module Shard Cost, Reroll Shards — push toward completion

**Card labs**:
- Recharge Demon Mode (T14W60) — enables multiple DM activations per run
- Recharge Nuke — enables multiple Nuke activations per run
- Recharge Second Wind — enables multiple Second Wind triggers per run

**Lab Coin Discount** (Main category, 99 levels) — starts to matter here. Card mastery
lab costs are extremely high (reaching Q-range coin costs at higher levels). Lab Coin
Discount reduces coin cost on all labs including mastery research. Invest before
committing to heavy mastery research.

---

### Late Mid Game (T12–T15+)

More niche and build-specific. Prioritize tower weaknesses — if your bottleneck is
modules, run module labs; if it's damage, run attack labs.

**Attack labs** (continue):
- Damage, Crit Factor, Attack Speed toward max
- Super Crit Chance and Multiplier
- Max Rend Armor (reduces boss armor)
- Damage Per Meter (if Spotlight+ is unlocked — significant SL+ amplifier)

**Lab Coin Discount** — ongoing investment (99 levels); card mastery labs get progressively more expensive.

**UW damage labs** (now worth investing):
- Chain Lightning Shock Chance, Chain Thunder
- Swamp Radius, Swamp Stun
- Smart Missile labs
- UW-specific damage labs based on your active UWs

**Module labs**:
- Module Effect Bands — remove specific sub-stats from the reroll pool. Valuable once
  you are actively rerolling sub-stats and want to reduce RNG variance.
- Module Coin Cost — reduce coin cost on module leveling if costs are a bottleneck.

**Enemy labs**:
- Boss Health (reduces boss HP — extends boss survivability, more time for coin windows)
- Fast Enemy Speed — situational; affects pacing of elite waves

**Defense%** — if pushing for the 98% hard cap in tournament builds, resume investing
past level 23 here. Not a priority until you are close to that cap via other means.

## Lab Slot Assignment Strategy

With 5 slots, assign each to a category rather than running all labs in the same area.
Avoids hyperfocusing on one stat at the expense of compounding weaknesses elsewhere.

Example assignment structure:
- **Slot 1**: Economy (UW coin labs, Coins Per Kill, Lab Coin Discount)
- **Slot 2**: Attack (Damage, Crit Factor, Attack Speed)
- **Slot 3**: Modules (Shatter Shards, Reroll Shards, Drop Chance labs)
- **Slot 4**: UW-specific (UW damage labs, UW duration/cooldown labs)
- **Slot 5**: Perks / Utility / whatever is the current bottleneck

This is a template, not a rule — adjust based on your weakest area. If modules are
neglected, run 2–3 slots on module labs until caught up. The principle is balance across
categories so no single weakness compounds unchecked.

## Labs to Avoid or Limit

| Lab | Reason |
|-----|--------|
| Interest | Low ROI at all stages — other economy labs provide better return |
| Recovery Package Amount | No meaningful synergies; low ROI |
| Recovery Package Max | No meaningful synergies; low ROI |
| Defense Absolute | Scales poorly past T1 farming; retire around T2–T3 |
| Defense% past level 23 | Sharp diminishing returns; revisit only when close to 98% cap |

**Recovery Package Chance is different** — do not confuse it with Amount or Max. RPC has
direct synergy with the **Galaxy Compressor** module, which reduces UW cooldowns each time
a package is collected. Higher package spawn chance means faster UW cycling, and is
essential for builds targeting **perma Chrono Field (pCF)**. RPC is a high-priority lab
once Galaxy Compressor is part of your build.

## Using the tower-analyzer MCP

Always check live lab state before making prioritization recommendations.

| Tool | When to call |
|------|-------------|
| `get_lab_state` | Get current lab levels across all categories to identify gaps |
| `get_labs` | Browse the full lab catalog with descriptions and unlock requirements; pass `category` to filter to one category |
| `search_labs` | Resolve a specific lab by name — handles typos and word-order differences; always use before `get_lab_costs` when the user names a lab |
| `get_lab_slots` | Check which slots are active, what's researching, and idle slots |
| `get_lab_costs` | Get coin cost and duration per level for a specific lab (use lab `id` from `search_labs` result) |
| `get_lab_speed_affordability` | Check whether current cell income can sustain a higher speed multiplier |
| `get_currencies` | Verify coin balance before recommending expensive lab starts |
| `get_recent_runs` | Determine farming vs. tournament context to prioritize build-specific labs |

### Personalization Rules

- **Resolve lab names via `search_labs` first.** Never pass a user-supplied lab name
  directly to `get_lab_costs` — use `search_labs` to find the exact DB name and `id`,
  then pass the `id`. This handles typos, abbreviations, and word-order differences.
- **Never recommend a lab that is tier/wave locked.** Each lab returned by `get_labs`
  and `search_labs` includes an `unlock` field `{ tier, wave }`. Cross-reference against
  the player's current tier PB (`get_tier_pbs`) before recommending.
- **Check Lab Speed level first.** If it is low relative to game stage, that is almost
  always the highest-ROI recommendation before anything else.
- **Idle slots are always the biggest mistake.** If `get_lab_slots` shows an empty slot,
  something should be in it — even a low-priority lab is better than nothing.
- **Coin balance gates the recommendation.** A lab that costs 500T coins is not
  actionable if the player has 2T in the bank. Call `get_currencies` first.
- **Build context drives category priority.** A farming player needs economy labs; a
  tournament player about to push a milestone needs defense and UW labs. Confirm via
  `get_recent_runs` before recommending.
